# NOC Thoughts design brief

## Concept

**Operational grimoire:** a field engineer's terminal crossed with an arcane technical manuscript.

The visual language should feel futuristic and strange without becoming a neon arcade parody. Information remains primary; decoration should reinforce hierarchy, system state, and navigation.

## Visual principles

- Obsidian and blue-black surfaces instead of absolute black.
- A restrained mint/cyan signal color for active state and navigation.
- Ultraviolet for arcane or secondary emphasis.
- Warm amber used sparingly for warnings and human detail.
- Condensed display typography for headlines, neutral sans-serif for reading, monospace for metadata.
- Circuit grids, geometric sigils, clipped panel corners, and fine technical rules.
- Large editorial typography balanced by quiet, readable article pages.
- Glows are reserved for status and focus—not placed around every element.

## Experience principles

- Fast static pages with progressive enhancement.
- Excellent long-form reading and code-block ergonomics.
- Keyboard-visible focus, semantic landmarks, strong contrast, and reduced-motion support.
- A compact mobile experience, not a shrunken desktop dashboard.
- No decorative effect may interfere with screenshots, commands, or technical prose.

## Required page families

- Home / recent transmissions
- Long-form post with sticky table of contents
- Posts-by-year archive
- Category and tag indexes
- Search
- About / operator profile
- Privacy policy
- 404 / lost signal

## Future visual assets

- Primary NOC Thoughts sigil/logo
- Default social sharing card
- Favicon and application-icon family
- Optional category glyphs
- Subtle background circuit/sigil texture

Brand assets should be original, locally stored, and usable without third-party services.
